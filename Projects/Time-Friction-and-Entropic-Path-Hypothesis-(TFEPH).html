Time Friction and Entropic Path Hypothesis (TFEPH)

“Time is not merely linear; it is quantum friction interacting with matter, shaping the fundamental limits of speed and gravity.”

This hypothesis proposes a new perspective where electromagnetic (EM) waves follow paths of least entropy, and where time itself acts as a frictional force at the quantum level — an effect missing in classical equations that limits massive bodies from reaching light speed.

Core Hypothesis

Traditional relativistic models treat time as a linear dimension without frictional resistance. TFEPH challenges this by framing time as the frictional interaction between quantum matter and the entropy flow within the fabric of space. In this model:

  • EM waves do not simply propagate through space; they traverse the least entropic path, analogous to geodesics but shaped by entropy gradients.
  • Time's friction causes differential resistance depending on mass and quantum state, explaining why massive bodies experience increasing resistance near light speed.
  • The classical light-speed gravity equation omits a time friction variable, which when accounted for, reveals why subatomic particles (electrons, protons) can approach or maintain light-speed propagation, while macroscopic masses cannot.

Theoretical Foundations

This approach synthesizes elements from quantum mechanics, thermodynamics, and relativity into a cohesive framework:

  • Entropy as a directional field: Entropy gradients guide energy flows, with EM waves naturally following paths minimizing total entropy production.
  • Time as friction: On a quantum scale, time manifests as resistance arising from entropy exchange, slowing propagation of massive bodies.
  • Mass-dependent velocity limits: Large mass systems interact more intensely with time friction, imposing a natural speed cap below light speed, unlike near-massless particles.

Mathematically, the classical relativistic velocity limit v < c emerges not only from invariant speed but also from a frictional term f_t representing entropic time resistance:

F_total = F_gravity - f_t(m, quantum_state)

Where f_t increases sharply with mass and internal quantum complexity.
