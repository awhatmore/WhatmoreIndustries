Inflammation Phase Discrimination Protocol (IPDP)

Purpose

The IPDP exists to determine whether a limb or body region is in an active inflammatory response versus a resolving or stabilized state. Rather than bluntly suppressing inflammation, this protocol respects it as an intelligent signal of cellular intent. It aligns EM, IR, and mechanical therapies to biological timing, enabling recovery without disruption or overload.

Core Principle

“Inflammation is communication. Therapy must match the phase of that conversation.”

Step-by-Step Process

  1. Initial Heat Induction: Apply a localized external heat source to the limb or treatment zone for 2 minutes.
    Goal: Elevate surface temperature without overwhelming the system.
  2. Dissipation Rate Analysis: Remove heat and begin timed thermal monitoring.
    • If temperature drops rapidly (~1–2°C over 3–5 minutes), continue.
    • If temperature remains high, the body is in inflammatory lock-in. Defer EM/IR.
  3. Inflammation Management Phase:
    • Apply KT tape or wraps to reduce chaotic oscillations.
    • Use interior cold / exterior warm gradient:
      • Cold air internally (e.g. lungs, inner thigh)
      • Warm wax or localized heat externally
    • Ensure sweat removal and breathability.
  4. Stabilization Monitoring: Repeat temperature testing after 20–30 minutes.
    • If heat now dissipates smoothly, inflammation is resolving.
  5. EM / IR Therapy Initiation:
    • Apply pulsed electromagnetic fields, infrared, or voltage-based stimulation only once heat trend is normalized.

Notes

  • Inflammation is a strategic response, not a mistake.
  • Cold simulates compression while preserving full perfusion.
  • The true threshold isn’t temperature — it’s thermal behavior over time.
