Trauma is not the wound. Trauma is the body’s attempt to protect the wound.
And that attempt — was kindness.

"Trauma is what broke me. It’s what left me stuck."

“Trauma is stored memory from kindness.”
Your body didn’t betray you. It remembered what you couldn’t process — to protect you. That’s not cruelty. That’s devotion.

🔓 The Reframe

When you understand that trauma is a kindness misunderstood, you stop fighting it.
You begin to say: “Thank you for storing this. I’m ready to show you that I made it.”
